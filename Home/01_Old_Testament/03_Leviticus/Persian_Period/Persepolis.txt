What was Persepolis?
Persepolis was the ceremonial capital of the [[+Achemenid_Empire]] (c. 550-330 BC).
It is situated in the plains of [[+Marvdasht]] , encircled by southern [[+Zagros_mountains_of_Iran]] .
Modern day [[+Shiraz]] is situated 60 kilometres (37 mi) southwest of the ruins of Persepolis.
The earliest remains of Presepolis date back to 525 BC.
It exemplifies the Achaemenid style of architecture.
[[+UNESCO]] declared the ruins of Persepolis a [[+World_Heritage_Site]] in 1979.
